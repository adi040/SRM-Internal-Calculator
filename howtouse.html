How to Use the Calculator?

To convert your marks to percentages for joint courses:

  1. Find Total Marks: Determine the total marks of the test.
  2. Identify Weightage: Find the weightage of the test (e.g., 10%).
  3. Convert Weightage: Change the weightage percentage to decimal (e.g., 10% becomes 10/100 = 0.1).
  4. Calculate Weighted Marks: Multiply the total marks by the decimal value to get the weighted marks.
  5. Find Percentage: Divide the marks you scored by the weighted marks to get the percentage.

Example:

  • Let's say the total marks in Cycle Test 1 is 25, and the weightage is 10%. If you scored 24 marks in Cycle Test 1:

    • Total Marks: 25
    • Weightage: 10%
    • Convert Weightage: 10% = 0.1
    • Calculate Weighted Marks: 25 marks Ã— 0.1 = 2.5 weighted marks
    • Find Percentage: 24 marks (scored) / 2.5 (weighted marks) = 9.6%

    So, in this case, your percentage for Cycle Test 1 would be 9.6%.

* Disclaimer: Marks must be converted to percentages before calculations for joint courses.
